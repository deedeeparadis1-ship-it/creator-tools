HOW TO USE THIS SWIPE FILE

Five steps to your next great title

  1. Find your niche section below
  2. Choose a title template that fits your video topic
  3. Replace the brackets with your specific content
  4. Keep it under 60 characters — check the length before publishing
  5. Write 3 versions and pick the clearest, most specific one

The formula column shows the structure behind each title so you can create unlimited variations of your own.

THE 6 RULES

What makes a great YouTube title

Under 60 characters
Longer titles get cut off on mobile and in search results
Lead with the benefit
Put the most compelling part first — viewers read left to right
Be specific, not vague
"How to lose 10lbs" beats "How to lose weight" every time
Use a number when possible
5 tips, 3 mistakes, 7 steps — numbers create expectation and trust
Include your keyword
Use the words your viewer actually types into YouTube search
Make them feel it's for them
Name your audience directly — "for women over 40" filters in the right viewer
BONUS TIP

Take these further with AI

Every title template in this swipe file can be pasted into any AI tool to instantly generate dozens of variations tailored to your exact niche, audience, and voice. Think of the 50 titles here as the seed. AI is the multiplier.

Copy and paste this prompt into any AI tool:

I make YouTube videos for [your audience] about [your niche].
Here is a title template I want to use: [paste any title template]
My video topic is: [describe your specific video in one sentence].
Give me 10 variations of this title tailored to my niche. Keep each one under 60 characters. Make them specific, search-friendly, and written for someone over 40.
