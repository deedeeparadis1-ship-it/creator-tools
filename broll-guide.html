🎬 The 4 Types of B-Roll Shots

Type 01
Transition Shots
Connective tissue that moves the story forward. Walking out the door, getting in the car, closing the garage. One good shot is enough.
Type 02
Story Shots
The moments that actually matter. The arrival, the reveal, the thing you're teaching. These deserve the full three-shot treatment.
Type 03
Detail Shots
The close-up that pulls your viewer in. A hand on a door handle, fingers typing, a product being opened. Small moments that make B-roll feel intentional.
Type 04
POV Shots
The camera becomes your eyes. Walking toward something, opening a door, seeing a view for the first time. Great for reveals and emotional payoffs.

📐 The 3-Shot System

01
Wide
Where are we? Set the scene. Show the full space. Use this every time you arrive somewhere new.
02
Medium
What are you doing? Waist or chest up. The workhorse shot. Works for talking and demonstrating.
03
Close-Up
What's the detail? Hands, objects, the thing you're showing. Tells the viewer exactly where to look.
Every time you arrive somewhere new: Wide → Medium → Close-Up. That's the whole system.

🎥 Adding Movement to Your Shots

↔
Push In / Pull Out
Slowly move toward or away from your subject. Great for flowers, food, or any close-up shot.
⇄
Lateral Pan
Move the camera side to side slowly. Keeps static shots from feeling flat.
↕
Tilt Up / Tilt Down
Move the camera up or down slowly. Works great on shelves, walls, or anything vertical.
✋
Let the Scene Move
When there's natural movement, hold still. People walking by, wind in flowers, someone working.

💡 5 Tips to Make B-Roll Easy

1
Film constantly
Turn that camera on as much as possible. Small moments -- even one or two seconds -- can be the best clips in your video.
2
Keep it organized
Build a random B-roll folder on your computer. Label it, date it, and keep adding to it. B-roll doesn't have to be filmed the same day as your video.
3
Get multiple angles
Same shot, different perspective. Ask yourself: where's the most unexpected place I could put this camera? That's usually your best angle.
4
Plan your shots ahead of time
Use the printable shot list below. Know your shots before you hit record so you don't get home missing something important.
5
Have fun with it
If it feels like a job, you won't do it. Show your personality. Use your creativity. Be yourself and the B-roll will show it.
