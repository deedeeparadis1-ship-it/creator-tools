Dashboard — your home base
The first thing you see when you log in. Think of it as your daily briefing.
  • Latest video performance — see views, watch time, and subscribers from your most recent upload
  • Channel analytics snapshot — a quick summary of how your channel is performing this week
  • Recent comments — see the latest comments across all your videos
  • News & updates — YouTube announcements and tips from the Creator Insider team
Day 2 tip: Bookmark studio.youtube.com — you'll come here every time you want to upload, check stats, or reply to comments.
Content — all your videos in one place
This is where you manage every video you've ever uploaded.
  • Upload a video — click the blue Upload button (top right) to add a new video
  • Edit title & description — click on any video to update its info at any time
  • Visibility settings — set videos to Public, Unlisted, or Private before publishing
  • Thumbnails — upload a custom thumbnail for each video here
  • Schedule uploads — set a date and time for your video to go live automatically
Day 7 tip: You'll be back here on the final day to publish your very first video. Get familiar with it now.
Analytics — understanding your numbers
Don't worry about this on Day 2 — but here's what to know for later.
  • Views & watch time — how many people watched and for how long
  • Subscribers — how many people subscribed (and unsubscribed) over time
  • Reach — how people found your video (search, browse, suggested)
  • Audience — age, gender, and location of your viewers
  • Revenue — once monetized, this is where your earnings live
Beginner tip: Don't obsess over analytics in your first 30 days. Focus on publishing. The data only gets useful once you have videos out there.
Comments — your community hub
Every comment across all your videos, in one place. This is where you build relationships.
  • Reply to comments — respond to viewers directly from here
  • Like comments — acknowledge viewers without typing a reply
  • Filter by status — see comments held for review, likely spam, or all comments
  • Pin a comment — pin your own comment to the top of a video (great for links)
Growth tip: Reply to every comment for your first 50 videos. It signals to YouTube that your community is engaged — and it builds loyalty fast.
Subtitles — captions for your videos
YouTube auto-generates captions for every video. You can also upload your own here.
  • Auto-generated captions — YouTube creates these automatically — they're usually 80–90% accurate
  • Edit captions — fix any errors in the auto-generated text
  • Upload your own — paste in a transcript or SRT file for 100% accuracy
  • Add languages — reach international audiences by adding translated captions
Accessibility tip: Always check your captions before your video goes public. Names, places, and niche terms often get auto-corrected incorrectly.
Customization — your channel's look and feel
This is where you set up your banner, profile photo, and channel description. You'll be here today.
  • Layout tab — set your channel trailer and featured sections
  • Branding tab — upload your profile photo, banner image, and watermark
  • Basic Info tab — edit your channel name, handle, description, and links
  • Publish button — top right corner — always click this to save your changes
Day 2 action: Customization → Branding tab to upload your banner. Then Basic Info tab to paste your channel description. Hit Publish.
Settings — channel preferences
You won't need most of this on Day 2, but it's good to know where things live.
  • General — currency settings for monetization
  • Channel — country, keywords, and whether your channel is made for kids
  • Upload defaults — set default titles, descriptions, tags, and visibility for new uploads
  • Permissions — add a manager or editor to your channel
  • Community — set up comment filters and blocked words
Quick win: Go to Settings → Channel → Basic info and add keywords that describe your niche. This helps YouTube categorize your channel correctly.
Day 2 quick reference
Everything you need to complete today's setup tasks, step by step.
Upload your profile photo
Recommended: 800 x 800 px
  • 1
    Go to Customization → Branding tab
  • 2
    Click Upload under Profile photo
  • 3
    Use a clear, well-lit photo of your face
  • 4
    Click Done, then Publish
Upload your channel banner
Required: 2560 x 1440 px
  • 1
    Design in Canva — search "YouTube Banner"
  • 2
    Download as PNG or JPG
  • 3
    Go to Customization → Branding tab
  • 4
    Upload under Banner image, then Publish
Add your channel description
Use the generator tool!
  • 1
    Use the Description Generator to write it
  • 2
    Go to Customization → Basic Info tab
  • 3
    Paste into the Description field
  • 4
    Click Publish in the top right
Add your channel handle & links
Your @handle is your URL
  • 1
    Go to Customization → Basic Info tab
  • 2
    Set your @handle (e.g. @YourChannelName)
  • 3
    Add links to Instagram, website, etc.
  • 4
    Click Publish to save
