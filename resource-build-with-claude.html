Let Claude help you build the thing you'll sell.

You can build two kinds of products in Claude: an interactive one people use on a web page, or a PDF people download. Below are prompts for both. Paste one in, then answer Claude's questions as it asks them. The more you tell it, the more the product sounds like you.

How it works

Two kinds of products. Pick what fits.

Not sure which one yours is? The first prompt helps you decide. Here's the difference.

Interactive

A clickable tool, quiz, calculator, or worksheet people use on a web page. It feels modern and impressive, and it needs to be hosted online (covered below).

PDF

A guide, checklist, or workbook people download. Simple, familiar, and the easiest path. No hosting needed, you just upload it to your sales platform.

The prompts

Copy a prompt. Answer the questions.

Each prompt is built to make Claude interview you first, so what you end up with is actually yours, not generic. Jump to the one you need.

Prompt 1 — Not sure which format? Start here.

Claude helps you choose

Use this when you don't yet know if your product should be interactive or a PDF.

I want to create a digital product to sell. Before we build anything, help me decide whether it should be an interactive web tool or a downloadable PDF. Ask me questions one at a time about who it's for, the one problem it solves, and how my buyer would actually use it. After I answer, recommend the format that fits best and tell me why.
Prompt 2 — Build an interactive product.

A clickable tool, quiz, or worksheet

Use this to build something people click and use on a web page.

I want to build an interactive [tool / quiz / calculator / worksheet] that I can sell as a digital product. Here's what it's about: [describe it in a sentence or two]. Before you build it, ask me questions one at a time about the outcome my buyer should get, what they'll click or fill in, and the look and feel I want. Once you understand it, build it as a single HTML file I can host. Keep it simple, clean, and easy for a non-techy person to use.
Prompt 3 — Build a PDF product.

A guide, checklist, or workbook

Use this to build something people download and keep.

I want to create a downloadable [guide / checklist / workbook] that I can sell as a PDF. Here's the topic: [describe it]. Before writing it, ask me questions one at a time about the one outcome my buyer gets, the steps to get there, and my tone of voice. Then write it section by section, checking with me as you go. Keep it clear, warm, and easy to follow, with no fluff.
Prompt 4 — Make it sound like you.

Rewrite it in your voice

Use this once you have a draft, to turn it from generic into yours.

Here's a sample of how I write: [paste a few sentences you've written, like a video script or an email]. Rewrite my product in this voice. Keep it warm and plain, use contractions, and cut anything that sounds stiff or salesy.
Prompt 5 — Polish it before you sell.

Claude checks it like a buyer

Use this last, as a final once-over before you put it up for sale.

Read through my finished product as if you're the buyer. Point out anything confusing, missing, or that doesn't deliver the promise. Then give me a short, honest list of what to fix before I sell it. Don't rewrite my voice out of it.

Select any prompt above to copy it, then paste it into Claude and replace the pink brackets with your details.

Get the most out of it

  • Answer Claude's questions honestly and in detail. Vague answers get vague products.
  • You don't have to accept the first version. Ask for changes until it's right.
  • If something's off, just say so plainly. "Make this simpler" or "this isn't my voice" works fine.
Host it

Putting your interactive product online.

Hosting just means putting your file online so it has a link you can share or sell.

Made a PDF? You're done. Just upload it to your sales platform like any file and skip this part. This is only for the interactive HTML kind, which needs to live on the web.

Here are free places to host it, rated 1 to 10 on how techy they feel. 1 is "drag a file and you're done." 10 is "you basically need to be a developer."

2/ 10

Netlify Drop · easiest

Drag your file onto the page and it hands you a link in seconds. No account needed to start. The gentlest option.

2/ 10

Tiiny Host

Same easy idea, built for dropping one file and getting a link fast. Very beginner-friendly. The free version is more limited on how long your file stays up.

5/ 10

GitHub Pages

Free, reliable, and permanent, and it's what I use. It just has more steps: make an account, create a repository, upload your file, then switch on Pages in the settings. Doable, just not the gentlest first time.

6/ 10

Cloudflare Pages

Free and powerful, but the setup usually means connecting a code repository, which is more than most beginners want for one file. Great once you're comfortable.

Worth a peek (newest and easiest): A few brand-new tools like OneClickLive and host-html are built for exactly this moment. Paste the HTML your AI made and get a link in under 10 seconds. They're the simplest of all. Just know they're new, and some only keep your file up free for a trial period.
If you get stuck on the tech: This is the one genuinely techy step in the whole process, and that's okay. If any of it feels over your head, open your favorite AI tool, tell it which service you're using, and ask it to walk you through hosting your file step by step. It'll guide you through it. And if your product can simply be a PDF instead, that's the easier path, and there's no shame in taking it.
