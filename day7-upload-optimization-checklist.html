Before you upload
Have everything ready before you open YouTube Studio
Video file is exported and saved Must have
1080p minimum, MP4 format. Know exactly where it is on your device before you start.
Title is finalized — under 60 characters Must have
Copy and paste it somewhere handy — you will need it in the upload form.
Description is written Must have
First 2 lines: your hook and main keyword. Then expand with detail, links, and your affiliate disclosure if relevant.
Thumbnail is ready to upload Must have
Your custom Canva thumbnail from Day 6 — saved as a JPG or PNG. Do not use the auto-generated option.
Tags list is ready Must have
10–15 tags: your main keyword, related keywords, your niche, and your channel name. Write them out before you start.
You have taken a breath and you are ready Must have
This is a big moment. It is allowed to feel like one. Take a second, then let's go.

Tip: Write your title, description, and tags in a Notes doc or Google Doc first. Copying and pasting into YouTube Studio is much faster and less stressful than typing it all in the upload form.

⬆️
Upload & optimize in YouTube Studio
Go to studio.youtube.com → Create → Upload Videos
Video file uploaded Must have
Click Select Files and choose your MP4. YouTube will start processing — this can take a few minutes. Fill in the details while it processes.
Title added Must have
Paste your finalized title — the clearest, most specific version from Day 6.
Description added with keywords in first 2 lines Must have
The first 2 lines show before the "Show more" button — treat them like a second title. Keywords here help YouTube understand your content.
Custom thumbnail uploaded Must have
Select Upload thumbnail and choose your Canva file. Never use the auto-generated option — it almost never captures your best moment.
Tags added Must have
Click Show More to find the tags field. Paste your 10–15 tags. Separate each with a comma.
Category set Must have
Under Show More — choose the category that best matches your niche. Helps YouTube place your video in the right context.
Added to a playlist Optional
If you have a series in mind, create a playlist now and add this video. Playlists improve watch time and give new viewers a clear path to follow.
End screens added Optional
Add a Subscribe button end screen at minimum. Video Editor → End Screens — set them to appear in the last 5–20 seconds of your video.
Visibility set to Public — video published The moment
Set to Public and click Publish. Or schedule it if you want it to go live at a specific time. Either way — this is it. Press the button.

Done is better than perfect. If you have the essentials — title, description, thumbnail, tags — that is enough. You can always go back and add end screens, playlists, and cards after it is live. Publishing is the goal.

After you publish
The 24 hours after going live matter — here is what to do
First 30 minutes
Share everywhere
Post on Instagram, Facebook, your email list, and every community you are in — including this one. Early views signal to YouTube that your video is worth showing to more people.
First few hours
Reply to every comment
The first comments on your video matter. Reply to every single one — even if it is just a heart or a thank you. This builds community and tells the algorithm your video is engaging.
48 hours later
Check your analytics — once
Look at click-through rate, average view duration, and where viewers drop off. These three numbers tell you everything you need to know for video two. Then close the tab.
This week
Do not obsess over the numbers
Video one is a learning experience. The goal was to publish — and you did. A small number of views on your first video is completely normal and has nothing to do with your potential.
When you are ready
Plan video two
The second video is easier than the first. You have already done the hardest part. Go back to your saved ideas from Day 3 and start the process again.

Set your posting schedule
Choose a pace you can actually maintain — then commit to it

Consistency is not about posting every day. It is about showing up on a schedule your audience can count on — and that you can actually sustain alongside your real life.

Once a week
Ambitious but achievable. Great for rapid channel growth if you can maintain it.
Twice a month
A strong sustainable pace. Gives you time to plan, film, and edit without burning out.
Once a month
Completely valid. One high-quality video a month consistently beats weekly videos that stop after six weeks.

I have chosen a posting schedule I can realistically maintain
Write it somewhere visible — your phone notes, your planner, your mirror. Make it real.
My next filming date is in my calendar
Block it out like a meeting. If it is not scheduled it will not happen.

Dee Dee's rule: Start smaller than you think you need to. It is far better to post once a month for a year than to post weekly for six weeks and then disappear. Consistency always wins.
