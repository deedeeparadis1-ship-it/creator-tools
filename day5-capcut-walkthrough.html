Step 1 of 8 — Getting started
1
Step 1 of 8
Import your footage
Before you can edit anything you need to get your footage into CapCut. This takes less than two minutes.
  • 1
    Download and open CapCut — available free at capcut.com or in the App Store / Google Play
  • 2
    Click New Project on the home screen
  • 3
    Click Import and navigate to where your video file is saved
  • 4
    Select your footage and click Add — it will appear on the timeline at the bottom of the screen
  • 5
    Make sure your project is set to 1080p — click the resolution setting in the top right corner to check
  • 6
    Watch your full footage once before you cut anything — note the timestamps of mistakes, long pauses, and anything off-topic

Tip: Watching before cutting is the step most beginners skip — and it is the most important one. You will edit much faster when you know exactly what you are working with.

Step 2 of 8
Cut your footage
This is the most important editing step. Cutting mistakes and pauses is what transforms raw footage into a watchable video. Take your time here.
  • 1
    Drag the playhead (the white vertical line) to the start of a mistake or long pause
  • 2
    Click on your clip in the timeline to select it, then tap Split — this cuts the clip at that point
  • 3
    Move the playhead to the end of the section you want to remove, click Split again
  • 4
    Select the unwanted section between the two cuts and press Delete
  • 5
    Repeat for every mistake, long pause, filler word, and off-topic section in your footage
  • 6
    Watch the edited version back — your video should feel tighter and more energetic than the raw footage

What to cut: mistakes and stumbles, pauses longer than 1–2 seconds, filler words like "um" and "so", anything that repeats information already covered, and sections where your energy drops noticeably.

Don't over-cut: a small pause here and there feels natural. Cutting every single breath makes your video feel rushed and exhausting to watch. Leave the natural rhythm in.

Step 3 of 8
Enhance your audio
Good audio is what separates a professional-feeling video from an amateur one. CapCut has built-in tools that make this easier than you think.
  • 1
    Select your main clip in the timeline, then tap Audio in the bottom menu
  • 2
    Turn on Noise Reduction — this removes background hum, air conditioning, and room noise automatically
  • 3
    Check your volume level — your voice should be clearly audible without distorting. Aim for a consistent level throughout
  • 4
    Use Auto Volume if your levels vary between sections — CapCut will balance them out automatically
  • 5
    Play back your audio with headphones if you have them — listen for anything that sounds off

Tip: If your audio still sounds echoey after noise reduction, try re-recording in a smaller room with softer furnishings — a closet full of clothes is genuinely one of the best recording spaces in a house.

Step 4 of 8
Add background music (optional)
Background music adds warmth and energy to your video. It is optional — but done right, it makes a real difference. The key word is subtle.
  • 1
    Tap Audio then Music in the bottom toolbar
  • 2
    Browse CapCut's built-in library — all tracks are royalty-free and safe to use on YouTube
  • 3
    Alternatively use the YouTube Audio Library (free at studio.youtube.com → Audio Library) — download a track and import it into CapCut
  • 4
    Choose something instrumental — no lyrics. Lyrics compete with your voice and distract viewers
  • 5
    Set the music volume to 10–20% of your voice level — it should be felt, not heard
  • 6
    Use fade in at the start and fade out at the end so the music does not cut abruptly

Copyright warning: Never use popular songs — even for a few seconds. YouTube's Content ID system will flag your video and it could be muted or removed. Stick to royalty-free music only.

Dee Dee's tip: If in doubt, skip the music for your first video. A clean, well-edited voice-only video is better than a video where the music competes with your message.

Step 5 of 8
Add text and graphics
Text on screen reinforces key points, keeps viewers engaged, and makes your video more accessible. Keep it clean and use it sparingly.
  • 1
    Tap Text in the bottom toolbar, then Add Text
  • 2
    Add a title card at the very beginning of your video — your video title or topic in large, readable text
  • 3
    Add on-screen text to highlight your key points — keep each one to 5 words or fewer
  • 4
    Choose a clean, readable font — avoid cursive or decorative fonts that are hard to read at speed
  • 5
    Make sure text contrasts with your background — white text with a dark shadow or outline works on most backgrounds
  • 6
    Add a CTA text card at the end of your video — "Subscribe for more" or "Watch Day 2 next"
  • 7
    Use Auto Captions under the Text menu — CapCut will automatically transcribe your speech into subtitles

Tip: Auto Captions is one of CapCut's best features. Adding captions makes your video accessible to more viewers, increases watch time, and helps people watch in environments where they can't use sound.

Step 6 of 8
Add transitions
Transitions connect your cuts smoothly. Less is more here — simple and clean always beats flashy and distracting.
  • 1
    Look for the small white diamond icon between clips on your timeline — tap it to add a transition
  • 2
    For most cuts between sections use a simple fade or dissolve — these are smooth and professional
  • 3
    Keep transition duration to 0.3–0.5 seconds — any longer feels slow and drawn out
  • 4
    For cuts within the same section — like between two sentences — no transition is needed. A clean cut is completely fine
  • 5
    Avoid flashy or spinning transitions — they pull attention away from your content and look dated

Simple rule: if you notice the transition, it is probably too much. The best transitions are the ones your viewer does not consciously register.

Step 7 of 8
Color correction
You do not need to be a colorist. For your first video, simple brightness and contrast adjustments are all you need to look polished and professional.
  • 1
    Select your main clip, then tap Adjust in the bottom menu
  • 2
    If your footage looks dark, increase Brightness slightly — keep it subtle
  • 3
    Increase Contrast slightly to make your image pop — again, less is more
  • 4
    If colors look washed out, increase Saturation slightly — a small boost goes a long way
  • 5
    Use a Filter if you want a consistent look — tap Filters and choose something subtle and warm. Avoid heavy, dramatic filters
  • 6
    Make sure your skin tone looks natural — if it looks too orange or too yellow, reduce Warmth slightly

Dee Dee's tip: The best color correction is the kind no one notices. Your goal is for your footage to look natural and bright — not processed. When in doubt, make smaller adjustments than you think you need.

Step 8 of 8 — Final step
Review and export
You are almost done. Watch your full edit one final time before you export — catching something now is much easier than re-exporting later.
  • 1
    Watch your full edit from start to finish — at least once, ideally twice. Check for awkward cuts, audio issues, and any text errors
  • 2
    Make any final tweaks, then tap the Export button in the top right corner
  • 3
    Set resolution to 1080p — this is the minimum for YouTube. If your footage was shot in 4K, export in 4K
  • 4
    Set frame rate to match your footage — 30fps is standard for talking head videos
  • 5
    Wait for the export to complete, then save the file somewhere you can easily find it for Day 6
