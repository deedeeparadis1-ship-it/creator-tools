Day 1 — YouTube Challenge

Find your why,
find your channel.

Work through these four exercises to build your foundation.
Take your time — your answers are private and stay in your browser.

Exercise 1 of 4
Finding your why
Your "why" is the fuel that keeps you going when views are low and motivation is hard. The 5 Whys technique helps you dig past the surface answer to find what truly drives you.

Your deepest why usually lives right here. Read your last answer back slowly. That's the real reason. Now write it as one clear, powerful statement you could say out loud.

No wrong answers here.
Exercise 2 of 4
Mindset check
The biggest thing that stops people from starting isn't skill — it's fear. Let's name yours and flip it before it stops you.
Exercise 3 of 4
Finding your niche
The best niches sit at the intersection of what you love, what you know, and what people are already searching for. Don't try to be for everyone — be everything to someone.

Select the broad category that best fits your channel (you can pick up to two):

Health & Fitness Personal Finance Parenting Business / Side Hustle Faith & Spirituality DIY & Home Food & Cooking Beauty & Style Relationships Education & Learning Tech & Gaming Travel & Lifestyle Mental Health & Wellness Creative Arts Mindset & Personal Growth
Exercise 4 of 4
Choosing your channel name
A great name is easy to remember, easy to search, and feels like you. It doesn't have to be perfect today — but let's get you to something you love.

These are jumping-off points — tap one to use it, or let them spark your own idea. The best name often comes from combining two of these.
