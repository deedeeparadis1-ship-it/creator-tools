Section 2 of 4 - Your numbers

Now let's look at your numbers.

This is where most people never go and it's usually where the answers are hiding. I'll tell you exactly where to find each one.

What is your click through rate (CTR)?
How to find it:
  1. Open YouTube Studio
  2. Click Analytics in the left menu
  3. Click the Reach tab at the top
  4. Look for Click Through Rate
  5. Set the date range to the last 90 days
Your CTR tells us how many people are actually clicking on your video when YouTube shows it to them. This is one of the first things I look at when a channel is stuck.
What is your average view duration?
How to find it:
  1. Stay in YouTube Studio
  2. Click Analytics in the left menu
  3. Click the Engagement tab at the top
  4. Look for Average View Duration
What is your average video length?
What is your total number of impressions over the last 90 days?
How to find it:
  1. Stay in YouTube Studio
  2. Click Analytics in the left menu
  3. Click the Reach tab at the top
  4. Look for Impressions
  5. Keep your date range at the last 90 days
If your impressions are low it means YouTube isn't showing your videos to many people yet. If your impressions are high but your views are low, that points straight to your titles and thumbnails.
How many subscribers have you gained in the last 90 days?
How to find it:
  1. Stay in YouTube Studio
  2. Click Analytics in the left menu
  3. Click the Audience tab at the top
  4. Look for Subscribers
Subscribers are YouTube's way of measuring trust. If people are watching but not subscribing it usually means your content isn't giving them a strong enough reason to come back.
